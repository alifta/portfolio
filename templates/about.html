About Me

Hey there, my name is Ali Farrokhtala and I am a Python developer, research assistant, and instructor assistant, from Vancouver British Columbia.

I started coding from the first year of my undergrad program in Computer Science using programming languages such as C and C++ and then I started using other languages during my research in graduate studies including Java (for Android development), Objective-C (for iOS development), Fortran (for scientific computing), and for the majority of most recent years I have been coding in Python for web development and scraping data and analyzing using machine learning or other well-stablish statistical methods.

Over the past few months, there have been several technologies being added to my to-learn list and that I am currently working on daily including JavaScript ecosystem including React,Node, and Vue.

Academically, I am writing my thesis to finish my PhD program my research focus is on graph theory and network science as the main area and designing and applying graph neural networks (GNN) to temporal complex network (such as idea exchange in user-user interaction via online social networks or face-to-face social interactions through out a pathogen spreading process) via using some of well-established Python frameworks such as NetworkX and PyG (PyTorch Geometric).
